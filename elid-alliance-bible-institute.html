üå± About Us

The vision for Elid Alliance Bible Institute was born during a time of reflection at the General Assembly held on June 3-7, 2025, in Puerto Princesa, Palawan. Its founder, Pastor Rhodnnie Jessnor Elid Austria, was inspired by a conversation among pastors about the positive impact of Bible schools on the growth of Alliance churches. This vision became even clearer during an alumni fellowship at City Alliance Church of Palawan, where a pastor shared the story of a student who was sent to Mindanao for ministry training but ended up settling there and did not return to serve in Palawan.

Determined to ensure the continuity of ministry in his home region, Pastor Austria set out to establish a Bible school in Luzon. His goal is to train and send out 100 students, so that the work of the ministry will continue for generations. With the encouragement and support of Rev. Faustino Aguilar Jr.‚Äîa retired worker and assistant pastor at Mutual Homes Gospel Christian Church‚ÄîPastor Austria was further inspired by Rev. Aguilar‚Äôs experience in helping to start PACT with former Bishop Dr. Valmike Apuzen, where church planting was a graduation requirement. In the same spirit, Elid Alliance Bible Institute is dedicated to training students to plant churches throughout Luzon.

The institute is named in honor of the late Rev. Reynato Elid Sr., Pastor Austria‚Äôs grandfather, who faithfully served as a pastor in Sulu and Tawi-Tawi for 25 years and was well-known within CAMACOP. The school is a tribute to his enduring legacy in ministry.

Pastor Rhodnnie Jessnor Elid Austria, the founder, has served as a pastor for nine years and is currently the head pastor of Mutual Homes Gospel Christian Church (CAMACOP), a position he has held for nearly five years. He is the author of Identity First, Destiny Second, a discipleship material used by his church for outreach and spiritual growth. In addition to his ministry work, Pastor Austria is an entrepreneur and a Certified Technical Analyst from the Society of Technical Analysts Philippines.

He believes that, unlike insects such as ants who specialize in a single task, humans are meant to be generalists‚Äîcontinually learning and growing in many areas. As the saying goes, ‚ÄúJack of all trades, master of none, but still better than a master of one.‚Äù This philosophy shapes the vision of Elid Alliance Bible Institute: to equip future leaders who are well-rounded, adaptable, and ready to serve wherever they are called.
